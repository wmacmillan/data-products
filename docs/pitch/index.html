Pitch deck Structure and Narrative Guidebook

This is for a standalone deck that would also be used for pitching on video calls.

What are we doing?

Pitch decks actually vary little regardless of the narrative chosen or the more subtle use case for any given presentation. That structure, which will follow below, is, essentially, Monroe’s Motivating Sequence (MMS) plus forematter and an appendix. In actual practice, narrative changes are accomplished by changing one or two slides at most, for any given fundraising scenario. The goal should be to stamp out free thinking and creativity and maximize the ratio of $$$/pitch. The slide and number of slides per section should be considered unalterable.1

As a reminder, MMS is a (1) relevant story that exemplifies a (2) problem that you have a (3) unique solution for that you will (4) visualize for the listener such that they hear your (5) call to action in order to get engaged.

  1. Story
    • This should be a cold open, basically.
    • It should be human-centric, and can be personal to you.
    • The problem should be obvious to the point where it’s almost unnecessary to state it directly.
  2. Problem
    • Nevertheless, you should state it directly.
    • Make it really, really obvious.
  3. Solution
    • In a pitch, it’s noteworthy that the solution you’re presenting is actually your company as an investment opportunity and not your product as a solution to the problem presented. However, you should maintain the charade that it’s the potential customer’s problems you are solving.
  4. Visualization
    • The target here is to get them to really feel like they know how it’s used/what it’s like to invest.
  5. Call-to-Action
    • Be direct.

Deck Structure

The list number indicates slide order in the deck. For most items there is a 1:1 ratio of line item to number of slides.

Forematter

  1. Title slide
  2. Executive Summary (including Value Prop in one sentence)
  3. Team

Setup

  1. Story + Problem
  2. Problem Statement

Visualization

  1. Us as Solution
    1. Product
    2. Market for Product
  2. Product as Solution
  3. How the Product Works
  4. How the Product Solves the Problem
    1. Features
    2. What the product looks like
    3. User Testimonials
  5. How the Users Turn into Revenue
    1. Revenue Model
    2. Market Size
  6. How Users Will Be Gained (G2M)
    1. Growth Trajectory
    2. G2M strategy
  7. Costs of Gaining Users
  8. The Team that Will gain said Users

CTA

  1. What that Team Needs
    1. Guidance
    2. Partners
    3. Money
  2. How that Money will be spent
  3. How a listener can take action (specifics on ask)
  4. Close Slide(s)
    1. Value Prop
    2. Contact

Appendix Section of Deck

  1. Product Roadmap
  2. Slides that answer likely questions
  3. Slides that serve alterative narratives
  4. Other general Slides

  1. You could, of course, alter the number, but it should really only be done for purposes of showmanship (e.g. a dramatic reveal, nifty transition on the deck, etc.). Honestly, only if you’re doing some big presentation based off the deck should you change anything related to the structure.↩︎
